Bharatanatyam Arangetram

Bharatanatyam is a classical South Indian dance that combines intricate footwork (नृत्त , nritta) and soulful storytelling (अभिनय, abhinaya). It originated thousands of years ago, in the Natya Sastra, authored by the sage Bharata. This dance form is unique in its use of geometric precision, rhythmic complexity and a distinctive language of gestures set to classical Indian (carnatic) music.

Arangetram, from Tamil, represents the ஏற்றம் (etram, ascent) to the அரங்கம் (arangam, stage), by a dancer. In this concert-length solo public recital, the dancer demonstrates mastery of the Bharatanatyam மார்கம் (margam, the Way).

COVID protocol

Since we will have several attendees with elevated risk, including Manonmani's grandparents, we have come up with the following COVID protocol. We hope that it will allow us to balance having fun and staying safe.

Masks required indoors

We require all attendees to wear masks indoors during the performance. We will provide disposable surgical masks at the door in case you have forgotten yours.
The performers (Manonmani and the live orchestra) will be unmasked during the show. We will all take off our masks while enjoying light refreshments outdoors.

Vaccination required

We ask that all attendees be vaccinated to the extent that they are eligible. This means that, barring medical exemptions, 12+ should be triple-vaccinated and 5+ should be double vaccinated. We welcome children under 5, and hope that the mask requirement will serve to protect them.

Hand sanitizer

We will provide hand sanitizer at the entry and encourage you to use it often, particularly before partaking of the refreshments.
